# How to Communicate Using a Homing Pigeon

If you are tired of companies reading your private chats, it’s time to go back to the basics. Homing pigeons have been "sending DMs" for thousands of years without a single data breach. Follow this simple guide to start your own feathered network.

### Things You’ll Need

To begin, you will need a **Homing Pigeon**. It is important to note that you cannot use a common park bird; you require a specific "Homing" breed trained to recognize your house as its only "Home." You will also need **small paper and a pen** for your message, a **leg canister** (a tiny tube that clips onto the bird’s leg), and a supply of **birdseed** to keep your messenger "charged" and motivated.

-----

### Step 1: Raise Your Messenger at "Home"

Pigeons do not have a built-in GPS that lets them fly to any address; they only know how to go **back**. You must raise the pigeon at the exact location where you want to receive messages. This house becomes the bird's permanent "Home Base."

### Step 2: Transport the Bird to your Friend

When you want your friend to be able to "text" you, you must physically transport the bird in a cage to their house. As long as the bird is at your friend's location, it functions like a "Send" button waiting to be pressed.

### Step 3: Write and Attach the Message

When your friend has a secret to share, they should write the message on a very thin piece of paper and roll it up tightly. This roll is then placed inside the leg canister. It is vital to never use tape on the feathers, as it interferes with the bird's wings and is generally considered rude.

### Step 4: Release the Bird

Your friend simply opens a window and releases the bird. The pigeon will circle a few times to orient itself using the Earth’s magnetic field and then begin its flight toward your house.

### Step 5: Check Your "Inbox"

Keep an eye on your pigeon coop. When the bird arrives, provide it with water and snacks, remove the paper from the canister, and enjoy your 100% private, un-trackable message.

-----

### some quick concernations:

Because this is an analog system, there is **no real-time support**. If you ask "What do you want for dinner?", expect an answer in 3 to 5 hours. Furthermore, the **weather policy** is strict; pigeons dislike flying in thunderstorms, so if it is raining, your "internet" is effectively down. Regarding **privacy**, while the message is encrypted from hackers, it is susceptible to "Physical Firewalls",if a hawk catches the bird, the packet is lost.

oh and btw , Do not try to Reply using the same bird. The bird only knows how to fly one way. To get a reply, you must already have one of your friend's pigeons stationed at your house.
